What If: Surviving on Other Planets

Mars

Asphyxiation: Caused by a lack of oxygen and low atmospheric pressure compared to Earth.

Freezing: Due to its low temperatures (-60ºC).

Boiling: The low atmospheric pressure causes bodily fluids to start boiling.

How to avoid it? With advanced technology that simulates Earth's pressure, provides oxygen, controls temperature, and protects from radiation.

Venus

Incineration: Caused by surface temperatures exceeding 460ºC.

Implosion: Its pressure is 90 times that of Earth, crushing the human body.

Asphyxiation: Due to a carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere, making it impossible to breathe.

How to avoid it? Extremely advanced technology with floating habitats and climate control.

Mercury

Incineration or freezing: Due to Mercury's extreme temperature variations.

Asphyxiation: Caused by the lack of oxygen in its atmosphere.

Radiation: Due to proximity to the Sun and the lack of atmospheric protection.

How to survive? Advanced technology for mobile habitats with temperature control and radiation protection.

Jupiter

Implosion: Due to extreme atmospheric pressure, crushing the human body as you descend.

Radiation: Caused by Jupiter’s strong magnetic field, generating intense radiation.

Massive storms: With dangerous conditions on the planet.

How to survive? Floating habitats with radiation protection and advanced technology to mitigate storms.

Saturn

Implosion: Similar to Jupiter, atmospheric pressure would crush any living being.

Radiation: Slightly lower than Jupiter, but still lethal.

Strong winds: Exceeding 1,800 km/h.

How to survive? Floating cities with protection against pressure and radiation, and possibly relocating to one of Saturn’s moons.

Uranus

Freezing: Temperatures drop to -244ºC, freezing any human instantly.

Asphyxiation: Due to a toxic atmosphere filled with methane.

Strong winds: That would complicate any exploration efforts.

How to survive? Floating stations with advanced heating systems in special suits.

Neptune

Fast winds: Reaching up to 2,100 km/h, deadly without protection.

Freezing: With temperatures as low as -220ºC.

Asphyxiation: Due to its hydrogen, helium, and methane atmosphere.

How to survive? Floating habitats with protection against extreme winds and freezing temperatures.
